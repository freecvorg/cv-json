- Articles, talks, tutorials, and educational content may reference cv.json by name.

### Restricted use

Modifications, extensions, or forks that **diverge from the published schema** must NOT be presented as:

- "cv.json compliant"
- "cv.json compatible"
- "the cv.json standard"
- or any phrasing that implies conformance to the canonical schema.

A divergent fork is welcome — that's what MIT permits — but it needs a different name. Call it "MyCV", "ResumeJSON-X", whatever. Just not cv.json.

### Why this matters

The point of a trademark on a schema name is to keep the name meaningful. If anyone can call anything "cv.json compliant", the words stop telling consumers anything. Tooling vendors need to be able to trust that a document advertised as cv.json actually parses against the schema.
